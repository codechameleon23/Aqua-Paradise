Plan a Visit to the Gurkha Museum

The Gurkha Museum at Winchester tells the moving and unique story of Gurkha service to the British Crown and its people for 200 years. Gurkha graves are spread across the face of the earth in nearly every country in which Britain has fought – silent testament to Gurkha loyalty and courage.

At the Gurkha Museum you will find a fitting tribute to the ‘bravest of the brave most generous of the generous...’ As the Gurkha motto tells.

The Gurkha Museum hold regular events and lectures throughout the year that are open for everyone. For more details on our upcoming events visit our What’s On page.

Opening Times

Monday to Saturday 10am - 5pm (last entrance 4.30pm)
Open all year except, Sundays and 25th December to 1st January inclusive.

Entry to the Museum

Adults: £5.00 | Concessions: £4.00
Children (14 or under accompanied): Free
Brigade Members & Service Personnel: Free
Friends of The Gurkha Museum: Free
Group concessions available on request.

Accessability

Disabled parking, wheelchair access, lift to all floors, stairlift and toilet facilities available. Induction loop available.

Announcement of temporary closure

Following the latest Government guidance regarding COVID-19 and careful consideration of the current situation, The Gurkha Museum has decided to close its doors to the public in the interest of visitor and staff safety, effective immediately.

The Museum will remain closed until further notice but will continue to operate behind the scenes with reduced staff on call to answer queries during this time. We will continue to monitor the situation closely and will issue updates through our website and social media channels as soon as we become aware of further changes to our operations.

May we thank our Visitors, Friends and Supporters for their understanding and support through this difficult period.
