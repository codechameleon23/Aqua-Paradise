Total Hip Replacement

Total hip replacement is a very effective and successful operation for osteoarthritis of the hip. As with all joint replacement surgery, the indications for surgery are debilitating pain and loss of function and mobility. Total hip replacement can be undertaken in a number of different ways using different bearing surfaces. The implants can be cemented into the bone or coated with a bio-active material that allows the bone to grow into the implant. I undertake both cemented and uncemented hip replacement using up-to-date techniques with implants that have a proven track record of success. Ordinarily one would expect more than 90% of total hip replacements to last for ten years or longer.

The operation can be undertaken with a surgical approach to the side or the back of the hip joint. Following surgery the aim is to regain mobility as soon as possible. I was involved in the set-up of the advanced recovery programme at Salisbury Foundation Trust and we work towards fast rehabilitation and early discharge to home. The risk of fracture or dislocation is highest in the first few weeks and walking aids are necessary and most patients are unable to drive for six weeks following surgery. In younger patients I often use ceramic as the bearing surface and, in older patients, metal on plastic is the usual implant design used. The results of surgery are very good with low surgical re-attention rates and low readmission rates to hospital. I am involved in the use of the robotic arm to facilitate accurate placement of the implants into the bone.
