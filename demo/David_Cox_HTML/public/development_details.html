Robotic Arm Surgery

I am involved with the introduction of many new exiting developments in knee and hip replacement surgery. I have been trained in the use of the Mako robotic arm to undertake knee and hip replacement surgery. With the use of CT scans and computer navigation, the orientation and alignment of the implants can be improved. The robotic arm has a power tool attached to it and, with the surgeon’s guidance, it is used to resect the bone, ensuring that the implant sits perfectly in the patient.

I have also been trained in the use of patient specific implants for knee replacement. In this situation the patient has a pre-operative CT scan to accurately assess the anatomy of the knee and the customised implant is then manufactured specifically for their knee.

The use of robotics will have a dramatic effect on joint replacement by ensuring that the orientation of the implant is as close to perfect as possible and the bone resection is kept to a minimum. It is hoped that these improvements in surgical practice will lead to a greater longevity and survival of the implant.
