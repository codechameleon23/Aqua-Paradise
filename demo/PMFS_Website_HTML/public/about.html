About us

About PMFS Blood

Pigs Might Fly theatrical washable blood was first created by the award-winning motion picture industry veteran, Nick Dudman, who found that in the course of his work he would regularly be asked by Special Effects teams to provide them with realistic fake blood. With numerous make-up effects jobs coming his way, Nick made up his own supplies for his work and it wasn’t long before other make-up artists were asking him to supply them also.

The washability of this “blood” was the key to its success - other bloods on the market were notorious for staining, a costly problem on costumes, wigs and unprotected skin. Not just for make-up artists, Nick’s Pigs Might Fly Blood has long been the choice of Wardrobe, Wigs and Props masters too due to the ease of cleaning away from fabric and furnishings. From crime thrillers to hospital dramas, it gets everywhere.

Pigs Might Fly blood is regularly seen on the stage and screen: from blockbuster movies, to major TV series; from London’s West End to Broadway and beyond; in fact, it is used worldwide by some of the Industry’s biggest names and used on some of the world’s most famous actors.

Now further advanced by Pigs Might Fly South, the range of bloods available has expanded to include pumping/splash blood, wound filler and scratch gel, silicon/gelatine non-beading blood, colour variants and an edible ‘mouth-safe’ version, all of which are fully compliant with Cosmetic Regulations.

About Nick Dudman

The professional career of Nick Dudman has spanned four decades, from his initiation into the ingenious design and production of early make-up effects for Lucasfilm’s ‘The Empire Strikes Back’ to his collaborative leadership at the helm of many of today’s best creature effects’ crews.

Nick’s Film and TV credits are extensive – many of the prosthetic makeup and creature effects seen on screen during the last 40 years have seen Nick being involved: Star Wars: Episode I, II, V & VI; Legend; Labyrinth; Willow; Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade; Batman (creating Jack Nicholson’s Joker); Alien 3; The Last of the Mohicans; Interview with the Vampire; Judge Dredd; The Fifth Element; The Mummy; The Mummy Returns; Harry Potter (1-7); Beowulf & Grendel; Penny Dreadful (Season 1-3); Carnival Row.

About PMFS

The Company was formed in 2012, when Nick and Sue Dudman’s Pigs Might Fly Limited no longer had the capacity to continue manufacturing blood products in their effects workshop in Cumbria. Nick’s work had become so busy that trying to maintain a steady flow of blood was an impossible task for Sue alone. A trip to Cornwall in early 2012 saw Nick and Sue meet up with their niece and her partner and the spark of an idea was created. Six months later and Pigs Might Fly South took over the manufacturing and distribution of the blood. A trading name of AJ Davies Limited, PMFSouth, based in Salisbury, Wiltshire, is run by Ant Davies and Lois Oakes (Nick and Sue’s niece); the blood is manufactured under licence from PMF Ltd, and whilst the day to day running of the business falls to Ant and Lois, Nick and Sue remain very much in the loop, ensuring that quality and attention to detail are maintained at all times.

Recent events in UK Politics have seen a need for further change and Pigs Might Fly South has now morphed into two divisions, albeit still very much linked. AJ Davies Ltd remains responsible for the manufacture of all of the blood products which continues at its site in Salisbury, as well as sales throughout the United Kingdom. A new company, AJD SFX Ltd, based in the Republic of Ireland, allows the distribution of products throughout the European Union to continue, as well as fulfilling orders from the rest of the world.

Compliance with European Cosmetic Regulation EC No:1223/2009 forms an essential part of the business strategy; the Regulation sets out safety guidelines as well as minimum standards that have to be achieved before Cosmetic products can be placed on the market within the EU. Pigs Might Fly blood has always been compliant with these cosmetic regulations and that will continue. As part of that Regulation, AJD SFX Ltd takes on the role of the Responsible Person within the EU, thus ensuring continued compliance and an assured supply route to end-Users.

Artificial blood is classified as a Cosmetic Product and therefore compliance with EC No:1223/2009 is obligatory. Sadly, some manufacturers choose to ignore these regulations, preferring instead to rely on luck, in the hope that no one will notice. The Regulation is there to ensure the quality of product and the safety of the User, so choosing whether or not to comply with it was never in any doubt for PMFSouth.
