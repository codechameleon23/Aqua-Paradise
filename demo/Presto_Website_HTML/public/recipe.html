Week 1 | Meal 1

Bangers and Mash.

A nice and easy one to get you going. Bangers and Mash is the perfect meal to have when the days are getting shorter and winter’s drawing in. This (sour) creamy mash is filling, comforting and delicious. It’s also one of the few recipes where instant gravy is preferable.

Prep time:
10 mins
Cook time:
20 mins
Serves:
1 Person

Ingredients:

2 x sausages

Half red onion

roughly chopped

3 x potatoes

peeled and roughly chopped

1 x tbsp sour cream

more if you like!

2 x tbsp of olive oil

1 x tbsp butter

Ingredients:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C and boil a kettle.
  2. Pour the water from the kettle into a pot, set the pot on the hob and turn the heat up to medium. Add a pinch of salt to the water.
  3. Once boiling again, put the potato chunks into the water.
  4. Heat the olive oil in a frying pan over a medium heat, once hot add the onions and leave to caramelise, stirring occasionally. You want them to get all caramelised and sticky, so don’t rush them. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Put the sausages in the oven, they should take about 15 minutes.
  6. When the potatoes are soft enough that you can easily push through them with a knife, take them off the heat and use a colander to drain them.
  7. Put the butter and sour cream in with the potatoes and start mashing them up. You can use a fork or potato masher, it doesn’t matter, but you want a smooth creamy consistency. Season with salt and pepper to your liking.
  8. Once the onions are soft and sticky, take them off the heat and set aside.
  9. The sausages will be done when they’re cooked all the way through. If you’re unsure then cutting one open and having a look will tell you. You shouldn’t see any pink in the centre of the sausage, if you do stick it back in the oven for 5 minutes.
